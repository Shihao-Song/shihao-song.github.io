About Me

I'm Dr. Shihao Song, a Senior Lead GPU Architect at NVIDIA and a passionate believer in education and advisory roles. Outside my primary role, I serve as an Affiliate Professor and Board Advisor at Drexel University, where I focus on students' long-term development through the supervision and creation of advanced, industry-aligned courses and academic-industry partnerships. Additionally, I advise stealth and early-stage startups on assessing early market targets and developing technical and strategic roadmaps, as well as support research projects aimed at benefiting the general public.

Recent Activities

  • Serving as TPC Member for Design Automation Conference (DAC) 2025 & Award Recognition
    I am honored to serve as a Technical Program Committee (TPC) member for the Design Automation Conference (DAC) 2025. This year, I was recognized as an Outstanding TPC Member for my contributions to the conference. Additionally, I had the privilege of serving as the Session Chair for the session titled "Smarter Compute, Faster Inference: Optimizing AI Systems on Edge" alongside Prof. Xiaoxuan Yang.

  • Instructor on Compiler Design at Drexel University (April 2025 - June 2025)
    Students will create their own programming language and write a compiler for it that translates this language all the way down to machine code which can be executed on the Intel IA-64 architecture. All major steps of the translation process will be covered: scanning, syntax analysis, code generation, and code optimization. The compiler will be developed using LLVM (https://llvm.org/), a very popular compiler infrastructure toolchain used by developers to create new programming languages or domain specific languages.
